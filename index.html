A Forward-Inverse Dynamic Game Framework for Enhanced Multi-Agent Trajectory Planning

Tianle Liu, Youchneg Niu, Jing Zeng, Shuo Li and JinMing Xu*
Zhejiang University
Under review

Abstract

Dynamic games serve as an effective framework for multi-agent trajectory planning; however, their resolution encounters two significant challenges: i) Traditional dynamic game models assume optimal agent behavior and prior knowl- edge of their goals, which is often unrealistic; ii) Inverse game theory has been used to infer agent goals from expert demonstrations, but struggles to bridge the gap between expert decisions with bounded rationality and model-driven solutions. To address these challenges, we propose a hybrid forward- inverse game framework to enhance the performance of multi- agent trajectory planning. In the inverse game, a neural network is employed to model agents’ complex cost functions, which is then updated by Inverse Reinforcement Learning (IRL) leveraging expert data. In the forward game, we integrate the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence and iterative linearization techniques to effectively bridge the gap between the expert decision and model-driven solution. Simulated and real-world experiments confirm the superiority of our approach against existing methods in multi-agent trajectory planning.
